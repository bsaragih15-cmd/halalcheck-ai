# MIND ID — Subsidiary Performance Metrics Framework & Tool Benchmark

*Working paper for the Portfolio Control Tower. Moves the dashboard from illustrative figures to a real, industry-grounded metric set for the MIND ID portfolio (PTBA · Inalum · Antam · Freeport Indonesia · Timah). All external figures are FY2024 unless noted, sourced from company disclosures and industry standards; see Sources. Confidential subsidiary actuals would replace the public reference figures shown here.*

---

## 1. The problem with a single scorecard

A coal mine, an aluminium smelter, a copper block-cave and a gold refinery are **not comparable on the same operating line**. Tonnes, ounces, kWh/t and strip ratios don't roll up. But a holding-company board still needs **one comparable view** plus the right **commodity-specific depth** underneath.

The benchmark of how the diversified majors (BHP, Rio Tinto, Glencore, Vale, Anglo American) actually report to their boards points to a consistent answer: a **small set of common value-driver KPIs that every asset reports the same way**, sitting on top of a **commodity-specific operating layer**. Every major frames the operating story as **two lines per asset: production vs guidance, and unit cost vs guidance** — then translates that into EBITDA, cash, returns and safety.

We adopt that structure as a **5-tier architecture.**

---

## 2. The metric architecture (5 tiers)

```
TIER 0  Group roll-up ............ the board's one screen (consolidated)
TIER 1  Common scorecard ......... same 12 KPIs for every subsidiary (comparable)
TIER 2  Commodity operating layer  the metrics that actually run each asset
TIER 3  Safety & ESG ............. SASB / ICMM / GISTM / PROPER (board-mandatory)
TIER 4  Capital & transformation .. growth projects + value programs
TIER 5  Indonesia & SOE overlay ... royalty/DMO/hilirisasi + BUMN governance
```

## TIER 0 — Group roll-up (the board's one screen)

What a diversified-miner board sees first. Consolidated, with each metric decomposable to subsidiary.

| Metric | Definition | Why it's here |
|---|---|---|
| **Group revenue & underlying EBITDA** | Consolidated, with EBITDA bridge by subsidiary | The headline. Majors lead with Underlying/Adjusted EBITDA (BHP $29.0bn, Rio $23.3bn, Glencore $14.4bn, Vale $15.4bn, Anglo $8.5bn FY24) |
| **Group EBITDA margin / mining margin** | EBITDA ÷ revenue | Anglo headlines "Group mining margin 30%"; Rio ~43%; BHP 54% |
| **Free cash flow & FCF conversion** | Operating cash flow − capex | The cash that funds dividends + state contribution |
| **Net debt and Net debt / EBITDA (gearing)** | Leverage vs a board-set target band | Every major manages to a band (BHP $5–15bn; Glencore $5bn cap; Vale $10–20bn; Anglo 1.3×) |
| **ROCE / ROIC** | Underlying EBIT ÷ avg capital employed | The capital-discipline KPI (Rio 18%, BHP 27%, Anglo 12%, Vale ROIC 16%) |
| **Capex: sustaining vs growth** | Split of capital deployed vs plan | Sustaining keeps assets alive; growth is the pipeline |
| **Group safety: fatalities + TRIFR** | See Tier 3 | Fatalities is a board-level zero-harm metric; non-negotiable |
| **Group Scope 1+2 emissions & intensity** | tCO₂e and tCO₂e per unit | Climate target tracking |
| **Contribution to the State** | Dividends + taxes + royalties (PNBP) + DHE retained | The SOE-specific bottom line (Tier 5) |
| **Cash-floor stress (breach probability)** | Existing Stress Test engine | What the Control Tower already does that the majors' tools don't |

## TIER 1 — Common subsidiary scorecard (the comparable layer)

**The 12 KPIs every subsidiary reports identically.** This is what makes Antam and PTBA comparable on one screen. Modeled directly on the majors' segment reporting.

| # | KPI | Definition / unit | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Production vs guidance** | Saleable output vs the year's guidance range (%) | The #1 operating line at every major. Status = within / above / below range |
| 2 | **Unit cost vs guidance** | C1 or AISC per unit vs guidance | The #2 line. Coal $/t · Al $/t · Ni $/t · Cu ¢/lb · Au $/oz |
| 3 | **Cost-curve quartile** | Asset's global cost position (Q1–Q4) | First-quartile = survives the whole price cycle; the single best resilience signal |
| 4 | **Realized price vs benchmark** | Achieved price vs reference index | HBA (coal), LME (Al/Ni/Cu), LBMA (gold) |
| 5 | **Revenue & EBITDA** | Subsidiary level, currency | Roll up to Tier 0 |
| 6 | **EBITDA margin** | EBITDA ÷ revenue (%) | Comparable profitability |
| 7 | **EBITDA vs budget** | Variance to board-approved budget | The current Monitor's main axis — keep it |
| 8 | **Sustaining + growth capex** | Capital deployed vs plan | Ties to Tier 4 |
| 9 | **Free cash flow** | Operating cash − capex | Who funds the group vs who consumes it |
| 10 | **ROCE** | EBIT ÷ capital employed (%) | Capital efficiency, comparable |
| 11 | **Safety: TRIFR + fatalities** | Tier 3 basis | Always on the scorecard |
| 12 | **Reserve life (LOM) + PROPER rating** | Years of reserves; environmental rating | Sustainability of the franchise |

> **Design rule:** Tier 1 is commodity-agnostic. A board member compares all five subsidiaries on these 12 without needing to know what a strip ratio is. Tier 2 is where the commodity expertise lives.

## TIER 3 — Safety & ESG (board-mandatory, standards-based)

Grounded in **SASB Metals & Mining (EM-MM codes), ICMM Mining Principles, GISTM, GHG Protocol, and Indonesia's PROPER.**

**Safety** *(state the hours-base: international = per 1,000,000 hrs; US MSHA/SASB = per 200,000 hrs):*
| Metric | Formula | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Fatalities** | Count of work-related deaths (zero-harm target) | ICMM P5 |
| **TRIFR** | (Fatalities+LTI+Restricted+Medical) × base ÷ hours worked | ICMM / OSHA |
| **LTIFR** | Lost-time injuries × base ÷ hours worked | ICMM |
| **Near-miss / HPI rate** | Leading indicator × base ÷ hours | SASB EM-MM-320a.1 |

**Environment / ESG:**
| Metric | Unit | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Scope 1 / 2 / 3 GHG + intensity | tCO₂e; tCO₂e/unit | GHG Protocol; SASB EM-MM-110a |
| Energy intensity; % renewable | GJ/t; % | SASB EM-MM-130a |
| Fresh water withdrawn/consumed; % in water-stress areas | '000 m³; % | SASB EM-MM-140a |
| Tailings inventory + **consequence classification** | table; Low→Extreme | SASB EM-MM-540a / **GISTM** |
| Land disturbed / rehabilitated | hectares; % | ICMM |
| **Closure / rehabilitation provision (ARO)** | currency | IAS 37 / IFRIC 1 |
| **PROPER rating** | Gold/Green/Blue/Red/Black | KLHK (Indonesia) |

> **GISTM consequence classification** is the single most board-relevant tailings KPI — it's consequence- (not probability-) based and sets the independent-review regime per facility. For a holding with multiple TSFs, this belongs on the board screen.

## TIER 5 — Indonesia regulatory & SOE governance overlay

What makes this an **Indonesian state-holding** scorecard, not a generic miner's.

**Regulatory metrics (PP 19/2025 + policy):**
| Metric | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Royalty / PNBP rate** (PP 19/2025, eff. Apr 2025) | Progressive on HBA/HMA: coal IUP ~4–14%, IUPK/ex-PKP2B 14–28%; nickel ore 14–19% (HMA $18k–31k/t); copper concentrate 7–10%; gold 7–16%; bauxite flat 5%; tin flat 3.75% |
| **DMO compliance (coal)** | 25% domestic (2025 target 239.7 Mt) at **$70/t cap** for power ($90/t for industry) |
| **HBA exposure** | Govt reference price, now mandatory minimum for coal **exports** since Mar 2025 (revised 2×/month) |
| **Hilirisasi / export-ban compliance** | Nickel ore ban (2020), bauxite ban (2023), copper-concentrate raw-export ban (Jan 2025) → domestic smelting obligation |
| **PROPER rating** | Per-site environmental rating Gold→Black (Tier 3) |
| **Divestment / IUPK status** | 51% local-ownership; PTFI = MIND ID 51.2% / FCX 48.8%, IUPK to 2041 |
| **TKDN (local content)** | Domestic-component %; ~30% common benchmark (mining gets case-by-case relaxations) |
| **DHE retention** (PP 8/2025, eff. Mar 2025) | **100%** of resource export proceeds held onshore for **12 months** (was 30% / 3 months) — a real working-capital constraint |

**SOE / BUMN governance KPIs** — the real Kementerian BUMN framework:

| Metric | Detail |
|---|---|
| **KPI Direksi — 5 perspectives** (PER-11/MBU/11/2020) | (1) Economic & social value for Indonesia, (2) Business-model innovation, (3) Technology leadership, (4) Investment increase, (5) Talent development. Set as a **Kontrak Manajemen** (multi-year) + annual contract, cascaded board→individual |
| **Contribution to State** | Dividends + taxes + royalties (PNBP) + DHE. *Note: since 2025, flagship-SOE dividends (incl. MIND ID) route to **Danantara** (UU 1/2025), not the state treasury directly* |
| **Planning anchors** | RJPP (5-yr strategic plan) → RKAP (annual work plan & budget, ministry-approved); KPI targets live in the RKAP package |
| **Health rating ("Tingkat Kesehatan")** | KEP-100/MBU/2002 → now PER-2/MBU/03/2023: financial / operational / administrative aspects → Sehat / Kurang Sehat / Tidak Sehat |
| **KPKU** | Baldrige-based performance-excellence assessment, mandatory for BUMN since 2012 (7 categories: leadership, strategy, customer, measurement, workforce, operations, results) |
| **AKHLAK culture index** | Amanah, Kompeten, Harmonis, Loyal, Adaptif, Kolaboratif (SE-7/MBU/07/2020); measured via the AKHLAK Culture Health Index (360° survey) |
| **TJSL / social** | PER-05/MBU/04/2021: social, environmental, economic, governance pillars |

> **Danantara is now the layer above MIND ID** (sovereign super-holding, UU 1/2025; MIND ID is one of its 7 flagships). Expect KPI emphasis to tilt further toward **value creation and investment returns** over direct dividend remittance. The Control Tower's Tier-0 "contribution to State" should show the Danantara routing explicitly.

## 4. Tool benchmark — what's out there, and the gap we fill

Mining performance software splits into four layers. **None of them is a board-level, cross-commodity, cash-stress control tower** — which is exactly the white space the Portfolio Control Tower occupies.

| Layer | Tools | What they do | Level | Gap for a holding board |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mine production / planning / asset** | RPMGlobal (XECUTE, **AMT** — benchmarks equipment cost across >60% of world fleet), Deswik, Datamine (**MineMarket** — mine-to-market CTRM), Hexagon (OP Pro fleet), Maptek (MaterialMRT), AspenTech (Mtell predictive maintenance), Micromine | Run a single mine: scheduling, fleet, reconciliation, maintenance | **Single mine / asset** | No cross-commodity rollup; no cash/board view |
| **Enterprise / BI / consolidation** | **SAP S/4HANA + Group Reporting + SAC** (multi-subsidiary financial consolidation), **Palantir Foundry** (Ontology digital twin — Rio Tinto), Power BI / Azure for Mining, Tableau | Financial consolidation; flexible dashboards; operational digital twin | **Enterprise / portfolio** | Powerful but generic; you build the metric model yourself; no mining cash-floor stress engine |
| **Market intelligence / cost curves** | **Wood Mackenzie**, **S&P Global Market Intelligence**, **McKinsey MineSpans/MineLens**, **CRU**, Visible Alpha | External benchmarking: place each asset on the **global cost curve / quartile**; emissions curves; productivity benchmarking | **Asset vs global peers** | Data/insight feeds, not an operating dashboard; subscription, periodic |
| **Majors' own ops centers** | Rio "Mine of the Future"/Perth Ops Centre, BHP IROC + Operating System, Anglo VOXEL + IROC Chile, Vale COI | Real-time **operational** control of fleets/plant/rail/port | **Operational, regional** | Explicitly *not* financial/strategic; runs the mine, not the portfolio |

**Synthesis — what the Control Tower should uniquely own:**
1. **Cross-commodity roll-up on one comparable scorecard** (Tier 1) — the majors do this in static results PDFs; no off-the-shelf tool does it live for a *diversified Indonesian holding*.
2. **Board-floor cash stress** (the existing Stress Test engine) — none of the four layers does scenario→cash→breach. This is the differentiator.
3. **Cost-curve quartile per asset** — ingest Wood Mackenzie/CRU/MineSpans as a *feed*, surface it as a Tier-1 KPI ("which assets survive a 40% price fall").
4. **Indonesia + SOE overlay** (Tier 5) — royalty/DMO/hilirisasi/PROPER/state-contribution. No global tool models this; it's MIND-ID-specific.
5. **Transformation value tracking** (Tier 4) — ties the RGP-style programs to the financials.

> **Recommended posture:** don't rebuild SAP or Wood Mackenzie. Position the Control Tower as the **board-level layer that sits on top of them** — consuming consolidation from SAP-type systems, cost-curve position from WoodMac/MineSpans, and operational truth from the ops centers, then adding the two things only it does: the **comparable cross-commodity scorecard** and the **cash-floor stress test**.

## 5. Recommendation — what to wire into the live Monitor

Concrete next step to upgrade `portfolio-monitor.html` from illustrative to this framework:

1. **Subsidiary tab → adopt the Tier-1 twelve.** Add to each card: *production vs guidance*, *unit cost vs guidance*, *cost-curve quartile chip*, *realized price vs benchmark*, alongside the existing EBITDA-vs-budget. Use the real commodity units (Mt, kt, TNi, Mlb, $/t, ¢/lb, $/oz).
2. **Add a Tier-2 drill-down** per subsidiary (click a card → the commodity-specific table above).
3. **Add Safety & ESG row** to the group KPI strip: fatalities, TRIFR, Scope 1+2, PROPER.
4. **Add the Indonesia/SOE overlay** as a 4th tab or a group-strip band: royalty rate in effect, DMO compliance, contribution-to-state, PROPER ratings.
5. **Wire cost-curve quartile** as the resilience signal that connects Monitor → Stress Test (a Q4 asset is the one the cash-floor breach will hit first).
6. **Data architecture:** define each KPI's source (management reporting for actuals/budget; WoodMac/MineSpans for quartile; ESDM for HBA/royalty; company HSE for safety). The dashboard is the presentation layer, not the system of record.
