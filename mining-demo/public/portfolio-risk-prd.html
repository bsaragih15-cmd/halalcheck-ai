PRD v1.0 - FY2025 baseline

Portfolio scenarios translated into holding cash decisions.

This PRD defines the v1 product requirements for a MIND ID portfolio scenario and risk engine. The engine federates a thin set of subsidiary risk signals, applies explainable common-factor scenarios, and converts portfolio stress into Holding Free Cash Flow at Risk, including debt principal repayments.

Status: publish-ready draft Prepared: 25 Jun 2026 Baseline: FY2025 annual reports Metric: Holding FCF-at-Risk
Headline decision Can holding cash absorb the portfolio shock?

The board-facing output is residual holding cash capacity after subsidiary upstreaming, obligations, debt principal, and management actions.

Portfolio scope PTBA, Antam, Inalum, Timah, Freeport

PTBA, Antam, Inalum, and Timah are quantified. Freeport remains visible as strategic JV exposure until validated upstreaming data is available.

Engine choice Common-factor portfolio model

Shared shocks drive correlated downside, revealing where diversification fails and where management action protects cash.

All numeric outputs in the prototype remain illustrative until FY2025 actuals, ownership, dividend receipts, and holding obligations are validated by MIND ID.

Executive summary

Build a holding-company risk cockpit, not another asset model.

MIND ID needs a portfolio-level engine that turns subsidiary risk signals into a board-grade view of holding-company cash capacity. The product should make it clear how common macro and commodity shocks flow through PTBA, Antam, Inalum, and Timah, how those shocks affect cash upstreaming, and whether holding-level obligations create a cash floor breach after management actions.

Primary metric Holding FCF-at-Risk

Includes opening cash, upstreaming, interest, principal, committed capex, equity injections, and mandatory outflows.

Primary behavior Scenario to action

Show gross downside, mitigation benefit, residual risk, and the owners responsible for each action.

Primary constraint Decision-grade transparency

Every output must carry confidence treatment so illustrative assumptions do not look like reported facts.

Problem and users

The board question is about cash resilience, not only EBITDA.

Existing subsidiary views can explain asset-level drivers, but they do not consistently answer whether MIND ID's holding cash position can absorb a combined portfolio shock. A coal downturn, nickel weakness, aluminium power-cost pressure, tin stress, regulatory change, FX movement, and refinancing squeeze can hit the portfolio in the same window. The product must expose that combined effect in language a board, CFO office, and transformation team can use.

Board and executive committee Needs a one-page risk answer

What changed, why cash is at risk, which decision is needed, and what management action protects the floor.

CFO and treasury office Needs funding pressure visibility

Debt principal, interest, refinancing room, liquidity buffer, and quarterly debt-wall timing.

Portfolio and transformation office Needs coordinated action ownership

Which levers sit at holding level, which sit with subsidiaries, and how fast each can preserve cash.

Goals and non-goals

V1 should be explainable, calibrated, and action-oriented.

Goal 01 Federate a thin data set

Request only the FY2025 fields required for holding-level decision making: revenue mix, EBITDA, capex, dividends, debt, ownership, payout behavior, and obligations.

Goal 02 Expose correlated downside

Use common factors so China demand, commodity cycle, FX, energy, regulation, and financing shocks can hit several assets together.

Goal 03 Recommend actions

Compare management packages by benefit, cost score, time to impact, owner, and whether P10 holding cash clears the floor.

Non-goals for v1

V1 will not replace subsidiary control rooms, provide audited forecasts, quantify Freeport upstreaming without validated JV data, run live ERP integration, or optimize portfolio valuation. It is a board-facing decision engine built on validated FY2025 baselines and transparent scenario assumptions.

Product experience

The screen flow should move from scenario setup to board decision.

V1 should feel like a holding-company war room. The first screen must answer how much cash is at risk, why, where it comes from, and which action package preserves the holding cash floor.

01 Select scenario

Choose preset shocks or adjust common-factor sliders for China, commodity cycle, FX, energy, carbon, regulation, and financing.

02 Read holding cash risk

Show residual Holding FCF, P10 downside, cash-floor breach probability, largest driver, and largest contributor.

03 Diagnose portfolio breakage

Display waterfall, distribution, cash runway, debt wall, and diversification-break map.

04 Choose mitigation

Compare holding and subsidiary levers, then apply the recommended package if it protects the cash floor.

05 Publish board brief

Generate a narrative covering what changed, why it matters, which decision is needed, and data caveats.

Risk model

The model starts with FY2025 actuals and applies transparent common factors.

The engine should remain explainable. It should translate shared factors into asset-specific impacts through calibrated sensitivity tables, convert those impacts into upstreaming outcomes, and then roll them through holding obligations.

P10 is the v1 downside case. P50 and P90 remain visible for context, and breach probability is calculated against the board cash floor or funding threshold.

Functional requirements

V1 requirements are organized around decision flow.

P0 requirements are needed for a credible publishable v1. P1 requirements create the board-room wow factor and should remain in scope for the executive demo. P2 requirements can follow once the baseline data and governance path are approved.

ID Requirement Priority Acceptance evidence
REQ-01 Provide preset scenarios and custom common-factor sliders for China demand, commodity cycle, USD/IDR, energy and power cost, carbon policy, Indonesia regulation, and financing pressure. P0 Changing any factor recomputes subsidiary impacts, holding FCF, distribution, breach probability, and narrative.
REQ-02 Calculate Holding FCF-at-Risk with debt principal repayments included in the headline metric. P0 Formula visibly includes opening cash, upstreaming, opex, interest, principal, capex, equity injections, and other mandatory outflows.
REQ-03 Quantify PTBA, Antam, Inalum, and Timah through FY2025 baseline cash bridges. P0 Each quantified subsidiary shows commodity, baseline cash, impact, residual cash, confidence tag, and driver note.
REQ-04 Keep Freeport Indonesia visible as strategic JV exposure while excluding it from quantified cash upstreaming. P0 Freeport appears in correlation and narrative views with a JV tag, and its quantified upstreaming value remains excluded.
REQ-05 Show gross downside, mitigation benefit, residual FCF-at-Risk, and the selected management levers. P0 Selecting or deselecting a lever updates mitigation benefit, residual FCF-at-Risk, P10, and breach probability.
REQ-06 Recommend an optimized action package based on cash-floor protection, total cost score, and number of levers. P1 The optimizer shows recommended P10, benefit, cost score, action owners, time to impact, and an apply action.
REQ-07 Show cash runway and debt wall across quarters so timing risk is visible. P1 Quarterly base cash, after-action cash, principal wall, and cash floor are visible in a single chart.

REQ-10 Export or publish a one-page scenario brief for board circulation. P2 User can generate a static shareable brief with scenario title, outputs, action list, caveats, and timestamp.
Data and governance

Confidence tags are a product requirement, not decoration.

The engine mixes reported FY2025 actuals, derived sensitivities, management assumptions, and illustrative prototype placeholders. V1 must make those distinctions visible so the model earns trust without pretending to have precision it does not yet have.

Acceptance criteria

A successful v1 changes how fast leadership can make a funding decision.

1
Scenario turnaround is under five minutes.

A user can open the tool, select a preset, adjust common factors, apply mitigation, and read the board narrative without editing a spreadsheet.

2
Debt principal is never hidden from the headline.

Holding FCF-at-Risk explicitly includes debt principal repayments and shows debt-wall timing in the cash runway.

3
Freeport treatment is consistent everywhere.

Freeport appears in portfolio risk visibility and narrative context, but no quantified cash upstreaming claim is made without validated JV data.

4
Action ownership is visible.

Each mitigation lever shows owner, time to impact, confidence or constraint, estimated benefit, and effect on residual risk.

5
Data caveats travel with the decision.

Board outputs include confidence notes for reported, derived, management, illustrative, and JV-excluded data.

Open questions

These inputs must be confirmed before production use.

Q
Which FY2025 source documents are authoritative?

Confirm annual-report availability and source fields for PTBA, Antam, Inalum, and Timah.

Q
What ownership and dividend receipts should be used?

Validate MIND ID ownership percentages, payout history, and actual FY2025 dividends or distributions.

Q
What is the approved board cash floor?

Confirm the liquidity threshold or funding trigger used for breach probability and optimizer feasibility.

Q
Which mitigation levers are governance-realistic?

Confirm which actions can be activated under SOE governance, lender constraints, and subsidiary autonomy.

Q
Can any Freeport data be shared?

If not, keep Freeport visible but excluded from quantified dividend-at-risk and holding cash upstreaming.
