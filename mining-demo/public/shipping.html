How to read this screen

What this is

A control tower for getting ore onto ships. It watches the whole chain — stockpile → jetty → barge → floating crane → ocean vessel — and finds the cheapest way to run it, trading demurrage (ships waiting) against stockpile carrying cost. The goal: ore never waits for ships, ships never wait for ore.

Read it in three bands

1Watch (top). The alert rail leads with the single highest-demurrage-risk vessel — name, why, the dollar exposure, and the fix. The KPI strip and value-chain ribbon show health; the red “OGV load · binding” node is today’s bottleneck.
2Plan (centre). The Schedule Gantt: laycan window (light band), confirmed loading (green, with %), AIS-predicted loading (hatched), demurrage overrun (red), and the TODAY line. Toggle Tide for the spring–neap curve and the DUKC low-water windows. The Live map tab shows real vessels, amber tugs/barges, and ETAs.
3Decide (right). Drag the four Scenario levers and the cost reacts instantly; press Generate AI Recommendation for the optimised plan, the value per year, and the concrete actions.

The four levers

Laycan buffer — safety time you hold (demurrage vs carrying). Floating cranes — loading capacity; the lever that relieves the binding constraint. Sailing draft — how deep you load, capped by the live tide (DUKC ceiling). Weather — auto-set from the live marine forecast; rough weather cuts crane capacity.

The cost bar

Annual controllable cost, split into demurrage, buffer carry, crane charter and tide/freight. The note flags when crane capacity is exceeded or the draft is over today’s tide ceiling.

Use it in one loop

See the ranked alert → open the vessel on the schedule or map → simulate a lever (or Generate) → act on the recommended buffer / crane / draft / hold.

Live chips (top-right)

Tidelive water level + next high/low. Ninickel reference (Indonesia HMA). Portlive congestion from AIS — ships at anchorage / inbound / average ETA. A green chip means it is fed by live data.

What’s live vs demo

Live: AIS vessel positions, tug/barge detection, OGV ETA & congestion; marine weather; tide & DUKC; the nickel HMA reference. Demo (synthetic but internally consistent): the example vessel line-up, stockpile cover and cost coefficients — these wire to the producer’s stockpile, grade, moisture and charter data on a pilot. Note: AIS carries positions, ETA and draught — not laycan or charter terms.
